Targeted Violence in America: Learning Overseas, Acting at Home

  • September 16, 2019

Samar Ali represented Millions of Conversations at the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace at a workshop on political violence. Samar collaborated with other leaders in the field, discussing political violence at home and abroad. The event concluded with a keynote speech in which Samar shared her own personal story.

Samar’s speech at Elon University’s First-Year Forum on global citizenship and innovation

  • October 8, 2019

Samar Ali addressed Elon’s first-year class, discussing her role as a global citizen and understanding her identity in America today. She also visited a professor’s specialty seminar on the events of 9/11 and explained its impact on American Muslim communities.

Free Trip to Egypt Film Screening

  • October 14, 2019

Millions of Conversations collaborated with Narrative 4, the American Muslim Advisory Council, Murfreesboro Muslim Youth, and others to host a screening of the film, Free Trip to Egypt, at Nashville’s Belcourt Theatre. Tarek Mounib, the film’s creator, came to Nashville to help host the event. Over 200 participants watched and joined the conversation with Tarek and other Nashville-area representatives to identify root causes of Islamophobia and misunderstanding in America today.

Communities Overcoming Extremism: The After Charlottesville Project” Final Report

  • October 29, 2019

Millions of Conversations joined other grassroots organizations and the former mayor of Charlottesville, Mike Signer, to produce a comprehensive report on political extremism in America. Samar also joined the discussion in a podcast series, outlining her connection to the campaign and its efforts.
